What's the Problem?

The problem itself has four key components:

Sharing photos online is now easier than ever

Social media offers an easy insight into other people's lives, leading to an increased pressure to be 'perfect'

Most devices are now capable of complex photo edits

The amount of photo editing information on the internet is overwhelming

Over Exposed Outdoor Photography

Harsh outdoor lighting conditions reduces detail

Incorrect Saturation

People commonly increase saturation to the point of colour shift

Bad Cropping of Landscape Shots

Bad cropping omits vital image elements

Why is this important?

Online Communication

As the majority of online communication is now done via photos (e.g. Instagram, Facebook), effectively connoting something via an image has never been more important

Intimidating tutorials

The majority of photography tutorials use jargon and technical language, which is intimidating for beginners and often puts many new users off.

Tutorials are outdated

Tutorials are often in inconvenient or outdated formats, often requiring the user to lug around a laptop or book

An area not tapped in to

More people than ever have access to mobile devices capable of showing rich information and performing complex edits, yet there are very few tutorial applications taking advantage of this

Why Me?

There are many reasons why I decided to tackle this project, however here are my five key reasons for doing so:

  1. Photography is a great passion of mine and has always been one of my main hobbies
  2. I enjoy helping others and teaching new things
  3. I am actively interested in finding new ways to harness technology in education, and so wanted to undertake a project based on this principle.
  4. I wanted to learn iOS/MacOS app development and apply it to a practical, real-world scenario
  5. I found the 'Image Processing' L2 submodule very interesting, so I wanted to further develop the skills gained from this submodule
